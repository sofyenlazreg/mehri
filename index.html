Braille writing is a genius idea that has not been developed

Braille's alphabet is a brilliant idea from a blind man who illuminated the minds of thousands and opened a world of culture and scientific knowledge that was the preserve of sighted people. However, this writing was not developed and did not receive sufficient attention due to selfishness, vanity, and immersion in a world driven by benefits and feasibility and that the rule of development followed in it is the aggressive law of the jungle. Reading letters in this writing depends on taking into account 6 fictitious points in two parallel vertical lines on the table of each line with 3 points and the figure drawn with prominent points on this canvas is what gives the meaning of the letter but its reading requires identifying the positions of the prominent points drawn over the fictitious ones. The letter "B" is read by touching two points drawn on the right side of the table, The blind beginner is told (b - 1,2) for example, he will imagine in his mind the location of the two points of the six fake points, and the matter becomes more complicated with regard to reading and writing other letters as "R" for example (1,2,3,5) and the letter F (2,4,5,6) - and so on for all letters and this is very stressful for the young child, especially since he has to evoke the arrangement of points and memorize that and not the rationality of the shape of the decree where he can not reason the image of the forms

My innovation ?

Converting Braille into a musical piece is possible by replacing points, separators and spaces with letters - this language will be greatly abbreviated in the simplified and modified writing I have prepared.

How it works

This is a modified Braille writing that enables us to ditch compeletely the use of numbers to determine the actual points of the six places where the punctuation can be made - for example to assign the points of the letter T we say (2,3,4,5), while 1 and 6 are two blank punctuation, and the modified writing is read in width Rather than length, letters are designated by order of points on the line, so we say about writing H for example: point, point over, point - and writing s: we say: two points, two points, a point - and we only have to define these three capabilities (point, point over, two points) Consequently, we get rid of numbers and consider the empty points, and the letter does not take from the space except what it occupies - in addition We note that we will reduce the use of points and dispense with considering empty positions in the points assigned to letters - and this book gives the possibility of converting them into lyric sounds (point la and point over mi and two points ré) - and in addition to all of this we will not need new tools, for example with regard to the student. Turn the board on which he writes, grasping the width instead of the length, and cutting out a line relative to the lines assigned to the letter - and the visually impaired (extremely weak) may find it easier to learn this writing by touch - because it is continuous on one line and does not exceed three points.
